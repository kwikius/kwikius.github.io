Crossbeam Construction

Hot Wire Cutting Device

The Crossbeam basic form is hot wired from blue foam.
This acts as a basis for building the structural carbon spar.
It is the spar that gives the crossbeam its stiffness.
But the foam has a part to play as it prevents the spar-web from buckling

Cutting the Slot for the spar

With the Foam being in two halves cutting the spar slot is easy.
This works on Balsa too- great for rudders and daggerboards.

Crossbeam spar construction

The carbon spar is similar to the construction used in a model glider wing.
The spar is made from carbon tow. This purest form of carbon fibre is cheap and also strongest.
The Web is carbon cloth with warp/weft at 45degrees, though it would be stiffer from two layers of unidirectional carbon.
The blue foam supports the web and stops it buckling.

Crossbeam before adding spar

The dark line is the carbon web.
This is split in two in the centre, allowing carbon tow spar to flow around the Pod/crossbeam joining tube.
After adding the carbon spar,the centre section only is reinforced with a tapered piece of 200g carbon cloth with warp/weft at 45degrees to the spar.

The carbon Spar Added

The carbon Spar makes the crossbeam very stiff.
Once the resin is cured tapping the spar makes it ring like a china plate.
