Foil Construction

Shaping the foil blanks

The blanks are made from 1/4" balsa (rudder and Daggerboard).
Shaping is more art than science. These my rules - take 'em or leave 'em!
  • Leading Edge radius say(1.5-2mm)Too sharp bad - turbulence/stalling.
  • Max thickness 30% aft.
  • Curve fairly fast at leading edge to straight aft 30%
  • Trailing Edge knife edge (tip: stiffen balsa near T.E with cyano)

Cutting Spar Slot

A runner is pinned to the foil blank.
a strip of A/O or W/D paper is stuck to a packer strip.
and then to a small sanding block.
Running it up and down cuts the slot.

T-foil alignment

A template is used to align the T-foil with the rudder hingeline.
(rudder hinge on Pod is aligned by setting up hull platform with spirit level)
Tip: a spirit level gives the ultimate reference plane!

Bonding the T-foil

A simple balsa right angle helps.
two dowels are drilled through the T-Foil into the bottom of the rudder.
The joint needs to be strong as the T-Foil can produce large downforce.

The various foil components

The 6mm carbon hull joiners are added to the daggerboard blanks.
These have been tapered so the carbon spar can run in.
Carbon spars added and faired.
Foils covered with 50g glass cloth.
Card with sellotape release agent can be D/S taped to the rear of foil during skinning per side to get a knife edge.
