Sailing Fast on a Reach

Caught by a gust... and jammed against a wave. The windward hull is way too high.
Increased heel means that the sail force is pushing down.
Ideally the boat should remain flat, with the windward hull just kissing the water.
The spray looks good... but is wasted energy.

Reaching Downwind

The upright attitude is due to the horizontal foil on the rudder which exerts considerable downforce. This provides stability at the expense of drag.
The boat would sail faster without it but would be more likely to flip in a gust.

Planing on flat water

Kwikis best sailing conditions.
The T-foil is not producing too much drag.
the boat is not heeled greatly.
The inward raked daggerboard provides some lift.

Flying a hull offwind

Keeping her like this is not easy. The burgee shows the apparent wind on the beam. The true wind will be more from the rear of the boat.
Though there is no wake she is travelling fast in this photo.

Whoops - nearly capsizing

This is how Kwiki capsizes.
I did recover this time. Kwiki is difficult to sail and reactions need to be fast. It is unwise to sail without access to a rescue boat. (Once capsized She wont right herself.)
